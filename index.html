Place satellites so every row and column has one, satellites never touch, and each planet’s number matches the satellites in its surrounding ring.

Play grid

Find the unique orbit.

Rules

  1. Place exactly one satellite in each row.
  2. Place exactly one satellite in each column.
  3. Satellites cannot touch, even diagonally.
  4. Each numbered planet counts satellites in the 8 surrounding cells.

Why it feels new

Orbit mixes the row-and-column elegance of Queens with a tiny Minesweeper-style clue ring, so the deduction is familiar but the flow feels novel.
